Cambodia's Tropical Rainforest Biome

Location on the Map

Areas like the Cardamom Mountains, Virachey National Park, and Tonle Sap Lake are part of Cambodia's tropical rainforest.

Image source: About Asia Travel

Living Things (Biotic Factors)

  • Plants: Tall trees, bamboo, and mangrove trees
  • Animals: Elephants, tigers, monkeys, birds like hornbills
  • Small Organisms: Mushrooms, bacteria

Non-Living Things (Abiotic Factors)

  • Climate: Hot and humid (25-35°C)
  • Rainfall: 1,500–3,000 mm/year
  • Soil: Poor in nutrients but decomposes quickly
  • Water: Rivers and floods (like Tonle Sap Lake)

What Makes This Biome Special?

  • Tonle Sap Lake: The lake grows and shrinks with the seasons.
  • Rare Animals: Tigers and crocodiles that live only here.
  • Angkor Wat: Old temples surrounded by forests.

Living and Non-Living Interactions

Predation: Tigers eat deer.
Competition: Trees compete for sunlight.
Mutual Help: Fig trees and fig wasps work together.
Decomposition: Fungi break down dead plants and animals to return nutrients to the soil.
